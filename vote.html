How to Vote

Simple Guide to Voting

Here's are simple steps to voting:

  1. Come in early or on time; know your local precinct’s schedule. If any instructions are given by a public official, follow them. Remember that you cannot bring campaign promotional material.
  2. Check if you’re at the right location by checking for your name on the voter’s list.
  3. After checking in, you will be given a ballot, a ballot secrecy folder, and a marking pen. There is no need to bring your own pen.
  4. Go to your designated room. Make sure that no one, even the public officials, sees your ballot.
    • When you shade the circles of the candidates you choose, make sure they are as neat as possible.
    • Do not make any extra markings on the ballot.
    • Do not fold, smear, tear, or tamper with the ballot.
    • Do not use your phone while you vote.
  5. After you are done, insert your ballot in the Vote Counting Machine (if you are not sure how to do so, ask a nearby official or clerk). Your thumbprint may be needed.
  6. Check if the names flashed are correct, then wait for an official or clerk to detach a physical receipt. Don’t pull on the receipt yourself.
  7. Ink will then be applied to your finger. You will be given your voter’s receipt (very important!)
  8. Follow the clerk’s instructions and drop your receipt in the Voter’s Receipt Receptacle.
  9. You are done voting!
