Trivia 1

Over 18,000 elective positions will be up for grabs in the May 2022 elections (based on the COMELEC, 18,180).

Trivia 2

National and local elections are done every second Monday of May, and have an interval of 6 to 3 years, according to the Constitution.

Trivia 3

The conditions to run for president aren’t very hard to fulfill. You need to be a 40 year old or older natural born Filipino who is a registered voter that can read and write. You also should have lived in the Philippines 10 years before the elections.

Trivia 4

The first national elections in the Philippines was held on September 16, 1935

Trivia 5

To vote, a Filipino must: be a Filipino citizen, be at least 18 years old on or before Election day (May 9, 2022), be a resident of the Philippines for at least one year and a resident of the place in which you intend to vote for at least 6 months.

Trivia 6

Domestic registered voters for the 2022 reach almost 65.7 million, with young Filipinos compromising 52% of that total (18-40 years old).

Trivia 7

    The Philippines has so many political parties, but here are the main nine:
  • Liberal Party (LP)
  • United Nationalist Alliance (UNA)
  • Nacionalista Party (NP)
  • Nationalist People’s Coalition (NPC)
  • Partido Demokratiko Pilipino-Lakas ng Bayan (PDP-Laban)
  • Kilusang Bagong Lipunan (KBL)
  • Lakas-Christian Muslim Democrats (Lakas CMD)
  • Aksyon Demokratiko
  • National Unity Party (NUP)

Trivia 8

First elected president: (technically Emilio Aguinaldo) Manuel Quezon

First elected Vice President: Sergio Osmeña.

Trivia 9

First female elected president: Corazon Aquino

First elected female Vice President: Gloria Macapagal Arroyo

First elected female senator: Geronima Tomelden-Pecson

Trivia 10

Voting is not a privilege, but rather an ingrained human right, which cannot be bought nor sold and which can never be taken away.
